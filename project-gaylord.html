Overview

Behind a handsome Memorial-area home sat a backyard that time had forgotten — a slab of cracked concrete, an aging kidney-shaped pool, and a deep, beautiful lot doing nothing for the family that owned it. They entertained year-round and wanted the back of the property to become the best room in the house, one without walls. Over seven months we cleared the yard and built a true outdoor pavilion designed to be used every month of a Houston year.

What We Did

The lot had everything going for it — depth, mature trees, privacy — and a backyard that ignored all of it. The brief was to create a genuine second living room outdoors: somewhere to cook, dine, lounge by a fire, and swim, finished to the same standard as the interior and durable enough for the Gulf Coast climate.

The Approach

We removed the old pool and slab entirely and re-planned the yard around a covered loggia with an ipe-slat ceiling and a limestone fireplace at one end. A full summer kitchen — built-in grill, vent hood, refrigeration, and stone counters — runs along the back, with a dining area beside it and a lounge oriented to the fire. Beyond the loggia, a new infinity-edge pool reads as a sheet of water that disappears toward the tree line.

Everything was specified for the outdoors and built to last: honed limestone decking laid for drainage, marine-grade finishes, integrated low-voltage lighting for dusk, and heaters tucked into the loggia ceiling so the room stays usable on a cool evening. The detailing matches the house, so the pavilion feels like an extension of the home rather than a backyard add-on.

"We use it every single day. Coffee in the morning, friends at night. It turned the back of our house into the place everyone actually wants to be."

The Result

An unused backyard is now the heart of the property — a resort-grade pavilion that cooks, gathers, and glows at dusk. The infinity pool draws the eye to the trees, the fireplace extends the season, and the family, by their own account, lives outdoors now in a way they never could before.

Scope of Work

  • Full demolition of the existing pool, deck, and concrete slab
  • Covered loggia with ipe-slat ceiling and integrated radiant heaters
  • Limestone outdoor fireplace and lounge
  • Full summer kitchen with built-in grill, vent hood, refrigeration, and stone counters
  • New infinity-edge pool and spa with automated equipment
  • Honed limestone decking engineered for drainage
  • Low-voltage landscape and architectural lighting
  • Drainage, irrigation, and landscape integration across the lot
