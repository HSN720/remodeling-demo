Overview

Beneath a renovated Heights home sat an afterthought: a dim, unfinished basement of bare concrete, exposed mechanicals, and overflow storage. The owner, a serious collector, had run out of room and wanted somewhere to keep — and show — a growing cellar. The brief was unusual and exacting: a true climate-controlled wine room that doubled as a tasting space dramatic enough to host a dinner in. Over five months we turned the most neglected room in the house into its most memorable.

What We Did

A wine room is half engineering, half theater. The space had to hold temperature and humidity perfectly for the life of the collection, and it had to feel like a destination — somewhere the owner would actually bring friends rather than just bottles. The low ceiling and raw concrete that made the basement feel like a liability became, with the right detailing, exactly what gave the room its intimacy.

The Approach

We built the room as a sealed, insulated envelope with a dedicated cooling and humidity system, then wrapped it in floor-to-ceiling walnut racking sized to the collection and lit to make the bottles glow. A wall of glass with a brass-framed door turns the cellar into something you look into as much as enter, and a stone-topped tasting table at the center anchors the room for six to sit and pour.

The palette is intentionally moody: dark walls, warm focused lighting, honed stone underfoot, and unlacquered brass that catches the light. Every rack, reveal, and fixture was detailed in our shop so a small, technically demanding room would feel less like equipment and more like a private club.

"It went from the room we hid to the room we show everyone. Now dinners end down here, not in the dining room."

The Result

The basement no one wanted to enter is now the room everyone gathers in. The collection is housed exactly as it should be, the glass enclosure turns the cellar into a piece of architecture, and the tasting table has quietly become the best seat in the house.

Scope of Work

  • Sealed, insulated cellar envelope with vapor barrier
  • Dedicated climate-control system for temperature and humidity
  • Floor-to-ceiling custom walnut racking for nearly 2,000 bottles
  • Glass enclosure with brass-framed entry door
  • Stone-topped central tasting table seating six
  • Honed stone flooring and dark plaster walls
  • Focused low-voltage display lighting and unlacquered brass details
  • New electrical, drainage, and mechanical integration
