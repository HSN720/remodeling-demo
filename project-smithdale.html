Overview

The primary bath in this Piney Point home had aged into a maze — a separate toilet room, a tiled garden tub no one used, a dim shower behind glass block, and a run of golden oak vanities that swallowed the light. The owners wanted calm: a single, uninterrupted spa they could move through barefoot at six in the morning. Over eleven weeks we removed every interior partition and rebuilt the room as one quiet, light-filled volume.

What We Did

The room had good bones and a generous window, but two decades of partitions had carved it into dim, single-purpose cells. The owners' brief was about feeling rather than fixtures: they wanted a bath that felt like a quiet hotel spa, easy to clean, warm underfoot, and organized around the soft north light that the original layout had walled off.

The Approach

We stripped the room to a single open plan and relocated the toilet to a discreet water closet, freeing the window wall for a sculptural freestanding stone tub. Behind a single pane of low-iron glass we built a generous wet room with a rain head and a hand-set marble bench, so the shower and tub share one continuous floor with no thresholds to break the calm.

The palette is deliberately quiet: honed Bianco Carrara in large format on the floors and walls, fluted white oak vanities with stone tops, brushed nickel fittings, and radiant heat beneath the entire floor. Nothing shouts; everything is meant to recede so the light and the stone can do the work.

"It's the first room I walk into every morning and the last at night, and it finally feels like the most peaceful place in the house."

The Result

The maze is gone. In its place is one serene, daylit spa where the tub sits in the window, the wet room disappears behind glass, and the floor is warm at dawn. The owners describe it as the quietest room in a busy house — exactly the brief.

Scope of Work

  • Full demolition and removal of all interior partitions and glass block
  • Reconfigured plan with a discreet water closet and open wet room
  • Frameless low-iron glass wet room with rain shower and marble bench
  • Sculptural freestanding stone tub set to the window
  • Honed large-format Bianco Carrara floors and walls
  • Custom fluted white oak vanities with stone tops and brushed nickel fittings
  • Radiant in-floor heating throughout
  • New plumbing, ventilation, and layered lighting on dimmers
