Overview

This family loved their 1930s West University cottage and their block — they simply needed more room and refused to leave either. A growing family had outpaced a one-story plan with a single cramped primary bedroom. Rather than move, they asked us to add up: a full second-story primary suite that would not look added. Over eight months we engineered the existing structure to carry a new floor and built an addition so faithful to the original that neighbors assumed it had always been there.

What We Did

The hardest additions are the ones no one notices. The clients' brief was as much about restraint as space: add a generous primary suite upstairs without disturbing the proportions, brick, and character that made the cottage worth keeping. In a neighborhood of tear-downs, they wanted proof that adding on could honor a house rather than overwhelm it.

The Approach

We began with structure — reinforcing the foundation and framing so the original walls could carry a full new floor — then designed the second story to match the cottage's roof pitch, eave lines, and window rhythm exactly. We sourced reclaimed brick to blend the new walls into the old, carried the existing trim profiles upstairs, and set the new windows in the same proportions so the elevation reads as one continuous design.

Inside, the new floor holds a light-filled primary suite: a vaulted bedroom, a spa bath, and dual dressing rooms, reached by a re-worked stair that ties the two levels together as if they had grown up together. Materials echo the period — oak floors, simple millwork, plaster — kept quiet and bright.

"Three different neighbors asked when we were going to start the addition. It was already finished. That's exactly what we wanted."

The Result

The cottage kept its soul and gained the room a growing family needed. From the street, the house looks as though it was always two stories; upstairs, a serene primary suite captures light the original plan never had. No one moved, and nothing of the home's character was lost.

Scope of Work

  • Foundation and structural reinforcement to carry a new second floor
  • Full second-story primary suite addition (bedroom, bath, dual dressing rooms)
  • Reclaimed brick and matched roof pitch, eaves, and window rhythm
  • Reworked staircase tying the original and new levels together
  • Period-faithful trim, oak flooring, and plaster finishes
  • New HVAC zone, electrical, and plumbing for the upper floor
  • Architectural lighting and integrated storage throughout the suite
  • Exterior restoration to blend new and original masonry
